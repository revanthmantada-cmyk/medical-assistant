Red Flags & Emergency Indicators

A collection of highly critical medical symptoms that require immediately contacting emergency health dispatchers. Learn warning signs to prevent delays in critical outcomes.

EMERGENCY

Cardiac Warning (Chest Pain)

Intense chest tightness, pressure or squeezing. May radiate down the left shoulder, neck, or jaw. Frequently accompanied by cold sweating, rapid heartbeat, and extreme anxiety.

Jaw pain Radiating pain Shortness of breath
EMERGENCY

Neurological F.A.S.T. Signs

Sudden facial drooping, arm drifting or asymmetric weakness, and slurred or garbled speech patterns. Every minute counts to minimize chronic tissue damage.

Facial weakness Slurred speech Balance loss
EMERGENCY

Severe Allergies (Anaphylaxis)

Swelling of the face, tongue, or pharynx after encountering triggers. Leads to extreme wheezing, low blood pressure drops, hives, and hives-induced rapid breathing limits.

Airway closing Swelling Hives
HIGH URGENCY

Uncontrolled Systemic Fever

Spiking high-grade fevers exceeding 103°F (39.4°C) that fail to settle with routine over-the-counter anti-fever medications. Frequently indicates complex biological infections.

Severe chills Sweats Delirium
HIGH URGENCY

Acute Rigid Abdomen

Severe abdominal cramping focused tightly on the lower-right side (appendicitis threat) or an abdomen that feels hard/rigid and extremely tender when soft hand pressure is released.

Rebound tenderness Appendicitis Rigidity
MODERATE

Cognitive Disruption & Dizziness

Abrupt feelings of severe spatial disorientation, room spinning (vertigo), extreme unsteadiness, or mental fog after long periods of heavy heat exposure or inadequate water hydration.

Vertigo Dehydration Confusion
